Looking for the right assistance can be a daunting task. While we always prefer to talk one on one and spend the time talking with you, we thought it might be helpful to post some of the most frequently asked questions that new clients have when looking for some assistance.

Click on a question below to see the answer

Am I coverd ?

Did you know that many extended health benefit plans cover the cost of nursing and homecare services? We will help you fill out the forms to get the care you or your loved one needs. We can help you find out if you qualify for any government funds to pay for the care you need. To find out if you’re covered, contact Leen On Me and we will help you determine what care you need, and will help you navigate the process to get you and your family started right away.

Will someone come for an initial consultation?

Although not everyone requires, or requests a visit, we usually recommend an initial consultation. It is often the smoothest way to start a service because it let’s the client and family voice any concerns, ask questions, as well as help us to match the right caregiver with the client’s needs and personality. It also gives the visiting co-coordinator a chance to make sure the environment is safe for the client and the caregiver, and to recommend any necessary changes or equipment, should any be needed.

Will It be the same caregiver each visit and will it be a set time?

This is one of the most common questions we hear when clients inquire about care. You as the client determine what days, and times are best for you. Our goal is to always have the same caregiver visit. We know that a bond is built between a caregiver and client. Your primary caregiver is the one who knows you best and is able to provide the best care. While we always like to train a back up caregiver, that is up to you and done at your pace. The only time you will have more than one caregiver, is if you are taking 24/7 care, where it is necessary to have one caregiver for daytime shifts, and another for evening.

What if I’m not happy with my caregiver?

Just call us and let us know things are not working out, and we can make an immediate change to your caregiver. While we try very hard to ensure that your caregiver is a good match for you, we understand that sometimes personalities clash. We will have another caregiver come to visit. We never judge, and will do this until you have the right match. Our goal to always ensure our clients happiness, and having a bond with your caregiver is a top priority.

What if I need to suspend care for a while?

ince you determine the days you require service, taking time off is never an issue. You are not billed for service not taken, or penalized for suspending care. For many of our clients they join as members without actively using services, which gives them the flexibility of using us only when service is needed. Some clients take service daily, while others may only use us once a month. We have clients who sign up for the year, and never use us, but enjoy the peace of mind that comes with being a member should sudden care be necessary.

What if my caregiver is ill or away?

We will always consult you first. All our prior booked services are guaranteed, and if your caregiver is away, we will always have someone there, unless you say otherwise. With most of our clients, we like to have someone trained as a back up caregiver in case of an emergency. This way they are already familiar with the routine, and you are not feeling like a stranger is in your home. We want you to always be comfortable with your caregiver, so having a back-up come in every so often to do care is sometimes an ideal situation. If your current caregiver seems to take a lot of days off, then we will give you the option of changing caregivers.

What happens if my needs change?

You, as the client direct your care. At the initial visit, the co-coordinator will take notes, and determine the best course of care for you. We pride ourselves in regularly updating our care plans with your regular caregiver, as well as speaking with you (the client) on a regular basis, to see if anything has changed. This is a good time to let us know of any changes, concerns, or any special requests you may have. We also suggest that you always call the office with any changes, rather than just tell your caregiver, as it is important that we keep our files up to date. It is really important that we always have the most current and up to date information, so that we can provide the best care.

What types of payment options are accepted ?

Leen On Me accepts many forms of payment. Contact us and our coordinator will answer any questions you have about payment in detail. Payments accepted are Cash, Cheque, Insurance coverage, Government funded, we also accept electronic payments like e-transfers, bank transfers.
